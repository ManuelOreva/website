Cape Coast


overview:

Cape Coast is a fishing port city situated in the Central Region of Ghana. The arterial language of Cape Coast is Fante. Founded by the people of Oguaa, Cape Coast is one of the most historical cities in Ghana. From the 16th century until Ghanaian independence, the city and fishing port changed hands between the British, the Portuguese, the Swedish, the Danish and the Dutch. It is enriched with thirty two (32) Festivals. The Cape Coast Castle which is now a World Heritage Site is a testament to European visits to this land of splendor.

The area is dominated by batholiths rock and is generally undulating with steep slopes. There are valleys of various streams between the hills with Kakum being the largest stream. The Kakum national Park derives its name from this stream.

The minor streams end in wetlands and the largest of which drains into the Fosu Lagoon at Bakano. Humid northern part of the district is ideal for the cultivation of various crops. As with many indigenous places in the world, the people of Cape Coast have a mascot. Crab is the city's mascot and a statue of one stands in the city centre.

The the biennial Panafest theatre, Cape Coast Centre for National Culture, the Oguaa Fetu Afahye festival (held on the first Saturday of September) and the Kakum National Park are but a few attractions to Cape Coast.

A theatre festival named the biennial Panafest was established in the 1980s, to contemplate the effects of the slave trade. Based on the ideas of W.E.B. Du Bois, there are academic lectures as well as music and dance performances.

Take a two days trip from Accra to Cape Coast to visit the main attractions: Elmina Castle, Kakum National Park and Cape Coast Castle. Have good time to relax by the beach.

We arrange special trip to the coast and back in Accra with very comfortable transportation and flexible timing. You will see the Cape coast and Elmina castles as well as the Kakum national park within a day. We can have a tailored made tour for you when we know your needs and expectations.

schedule:

Day 1 - Kakum National Park

  • At 6:00 A.M your tour guide will pick you up from a location agreed on the previous day.
  • The road trip is a three to four hour trip depending on traffic and weather conditions.
  • Our first stop will be at the Kakum National Park, home to forest elephants, bongo antelopes and primates like the Diana monkey. The Canopy Walkway, suspended 30 meters above the ground, provides treetop views of the forest.
  • There's the option of doing a 1h guided hiking through the forest at an extra fee.
  • Relax by the beach, take a walk around local shops, enjoy a drink and have dinner.

Day 2 - Elmina Castle and Cape Coast Castle

  • We will be heading to Elmina Castle. Here you will witness History. This trip will remind you of the dark sides of slavery and the lessons it leaves for current and future generations. Elmina Castle is the oldest European building in existence in the south of the Sahara.
  • We take a walk around this beautiful fishing village and witness the fishermen on their daily tasks with passion.
  • Stop by a local "chop bar" for lunch.
  • The last stop will be Cape Coast Castle. Cape Coast Castle is one of about forty "slave castles", or large commercial forts, built on the Gold Coast of West Africa by European traders. It was originally built by the Swedes for trade in timber and gold, but later used in the trans-Atlantic slave trade.
  • 6:00 P.M will be time to make our way back to Accra.
