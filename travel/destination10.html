Nzulezu


overview:

Nzulezo is a village built entirely on stilts on Amanzule river. It is constructed out of wood and raffia with one central walkway and to dozen houses on either side. The only way to get there is to travel 5km on the lake in a local canoe. The most striking feature at Nzulezo are the wooden accommodation facilities hanging some five feet above the lake level. As a tradition, visitors to the village are welcomed by the chief of the village. He will give a talk on the history of the place and answer any questions from tourist.

schedule:

  • There are no direct transports to Nzulezo. It’s about 6 Hours drive from Accra to Takoradi. From Takoradi to Nzulezu is about 3 Hours journey.
  • At 6:00 A.M your tour guide, will pick you up at your hotel. It's about 6 hours and half drive from Accra to Takoradi
  • From Takoradi we will take continue our journey to Banyin. That is an hour journey.
  • We will then board take a boat from Banyin to Nzulezu. It will take us 45 to 5o minutes to get to Nzulezu.
  • The people of Nzulezu are friendly and welcoming. We will have the experience of fishing on the local river and having a taste of their local palm wine drink.

important:

  • Hydration BackPacks
  • Water bottles
  • Mosquitoe Repellant
  • Sunscreen
  • Malaria Pills

It would help to have a travel Insurance package.
