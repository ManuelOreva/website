Mole National park

Enjoy an unforgettable wildlife viewing of African safari tours at Mole National park in Ghana.

Experience elephants, lions, leopards, cheetah and other wildlife animals in their natural habitat.

Discover the flora and fauna in their natural habitat.

Visit and interact with friendly tribes living in small villages in remote and off the beaten places.


overview:

Mole National Park is Ghana's largest national park, situated in the heart of the pristine Guinea Savannah Woodland ecosystem in northern Ghana. It covers an area of 4,577 square kilometres (kmÂ²) and provides the best wildlife experience.

In the western part of the park, the Konkori Escarpment has panoramic views and overlooks waterholes where animals drink. There are waterfalls along the Kparia and Polzen rivers. To the south, Larabanga village has a centuries-old, Sudanese-style mosque.

Mole National Park has the widest range of wildlife in Ghana and offers tourist an experience of Elephant, leopards and rare birds such as the white-backed vulture in the wild. In the company of tour guards, one can easily have a sight of monkeys, warthogs, bushbucks, antelope, baboons, and other smaller wildlife.

In the company of armed wildlife ranger, Walking safaris usually take place in the early morning (6am) and late afternoon (4pm). Another option is to go on a driving safari with an armed ranger. These can be for up to a full day and gives you the opportunity to cover a much wider area of the park.

schedule:

Day 1: Accra

You will be met at the Kotoka International Airport and driven to your hotel.

Day 2: Accra - Tamale - Mole National Park

  • You will be taken on a 6 hour drive from Accra to Tamale.
  • From Tamale we will embark on a a 3 hour drive to Mole National Park.
  • You will be transported to Mole Motel- a stunning location situated on the watering holes with a pleasant pool. You will start off with your late afternoon drive safari.
